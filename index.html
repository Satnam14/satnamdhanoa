Q. So why do you want to become a software developer?

I love engineering unique solutions to complex problems, and I've never found a better, more exciting avenue for doing that than writing software for the internet.

During high school, me and my friends started playing Counter Strike during sleepovers. On the very first night, my team lost all the matches. I’m a terrible loser. I had to be on the winning team and on top of all the leaderboards. I wanted to get better practice against harder opponents and I couldn't play online because I only had a 56kbps dial-up connection available.

I found that I could use Prolog as a scripting language to make bots smarter. Writing those scripts was a striking experience - I felt very energized and absorbed in the task. Ideas would come to me as I fell asleep, out of nowhere. I knew I had found a passion.

Choosing CS as my major wasn't a hard choice and I got to learn programming, databases, networks and a myriad of subjects in depth. App Academy taught me the very latest technologies being used in the industry.

Now, I'm just looking for a challenge.
